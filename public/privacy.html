Privacy Policy & Terms of Service

Effective date: May 2025 · Last updated: May 2025

Welcome to ShuHaul, the world's most exclusive hauling platform. By using this application you agree to these terms. Given that the entire fleet consists of one (1) Ford F-150 driven by one (1) person named Sheldon, these terms are mercifully short.

Privacy Policy

1. What We Collect

When you request a ride, ShuHaul collects the following information:

  • Your name, as you enter it in the booking form
  • Your approximate GPS coordinates or device location, used solely to center the map and communicate your pickup point to the driver
  • Your destination, as you type it
  • Your selected vehicle tier (X, Black, or Pool — they all result in the same truck)

We do not collect your email, payment details, social security number, blood type, or opinions on pineapple on pizza.

2. How We Use Your Data

The information you provide is used exclusively to send a single SMS notification to the driver (Sheldon) containing your name, pickup location, destination, and selected tier. This is the entire purpose of the backend.

We do not sell, share, rent, auction, barter, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties. The only third party involved is Twilio, which transmits the SMS, and they have their own privacy policy.

3. SMS Messaging

By submitting a ride request, you acknowledge that an SMS message will be sent to the driver on your behalf. You will not personally receive any SMS messages from ShuHaul. Message and data rates may apply to the driver's phone plan. ShuHaul is not responsible for Sheldon's carrier fees.

To stop receiving ride requests, Sheldon can reply STOP to any message.

4. Location Data

Your location is requested via the browser's Geolocation API and is used only to center the map on your position and populate the pickup field. Location data is transmitted to the server only when you confirm a ride request. It is not stored, logged, or retained after the SMS is sent.

5. Data Retention

We retain nothing. No database. No logs beyond what Cloudflare and Twilio maintain as part of their standard infrastructure. ShuHaul is a stateless application.

6. Cookies

ShuHaul does not use cookies, tracking pixels, analytics scripts, or any other surveillance technology. We don't even know you visited.

7. Children's Privacy

ShuHaul is not directed at children under 13. If a child under 13 is requesting a ride to Google I/O, that is impressive but beyond the scope of this policy.

8. Contact

Privacy questions can be directed to Sheldon directly, probably via the SMS thread he's already on.

Terms of Service

ShuHaul provides a request interface for arranging transportation from a single driver operating a single pickup truck. The service area is wherever Sheldon happens to be. Availability is subject to Sheldon's schedule, coffee stops, and navigational confidence.

2. No Guarantees on ETA

Estimated times of arrival displayed in the application are for entertainment purposes. ShuHaul makes no warranty, express or implied, that Sheldon will arrive within any stated timeframe. Historical ETA performance includes but is not limited to: missed turns, traffic delays, voluntary coffee procurement, and general existential uncertainty.

3. Vehicle Tiers

The ShuHaul X, ShuHaul Black, and ShuHaul Pool tiers are presented for illustrative purposes. All tiers result in the same vehicle. "Premium cabin seating with complimentary AC" is contingent on the front seat being unoccupied and the AC compressor continuing to function. ShuHaul Pool riders acknowledge the possible presence of cargo including but not limited to 3D printers, server equipment, and flat-pack furniture.

4. Pricing

All rides are complimentary. ShuHaul is not a commercial transportation service and does not charge fares. Tipping Sheldon in coffee is encouraged but not required.
